Game objective

What Is the Goal of Fruit Merge?

Fruit Merge is a casual physics puzzle game. You drop fruits into the container and place two matching fruits together. When identical fruits touch, they disappear and create the next fruit in the evolution chain.

Your goal is to unlock larger fruits, earn as many points as possible, and stop the stack from remaining above the danger line. The game continues as long as the container has enough safe space.

Core rule: different fruits do not merge. Only two fruits of the same type can create the next fruit.

Desktop controls

How to Play Fruit Merge on Desktop

Move the mouse across the game container to choose the horizontal drop position. The fruit at the top and the dotted vertical guide show where it will fall.

Click inside the game area to release the fruit. After a short delay, the next fruit becomes available. You can continue moving the pointer while earlier fruits settle and roll.

Mobile controls

How to Play Fruit Merge on Mobile

On mobile, tap the horizontal position where you want the fruit to fall. A single tap both selects the position and releases the fruit. You do not need to drag the fruit.

The top mobile HUD shows your score, best score, survival time, the next fruit, and the settings button. Open the settings menu to restart, change music, share your score, or exit immersive mode.

Matching rules

How Fruit Merging Works

Two identical fruits merge when their physical bodies touch. The matching pair is replaced by one fruit from the next level, your score increases, and the game displays a merge animation and score popup.

2 Cherries → 1 Strawberry → Grape → Orange

Larger merges award more points, but they also create fruits that occupy more space. A good run therefore needs both frequent merges and careful space management.

Planning ahead

Use the Next Fruit Preview

The Next panel shows the fruit you are currently placing and the second and third upcoming fruits. Check all three before every drop instead of reacting only to the fruit at the top of the container.

For example, when two upcoming fruits are the same, leave enough room to place them near each other. Planning several drops ahead helps you create faster merges and prevents isolated small fruits from becoming trapped.

Practical habit: look at the queue first, choose the destination second, and only then release the current fruit.

Game over rule

What Does the Danger Line Mean?

The red dashed line near the top of the container is the danger line. A falling fruit may briefly pass through this line without ending the game.

The game ends when settled fruits remain too high for too long. When the stack approaches the line, stop building vertical towers and look for merges that reduce the height of the pile.

Beginner strategy

Beginner Tips for a Higher Score

Keep the center open

Avoid blocking the middle with your largest fruits. An open center gives later drops more routes to reach matching fruits.

Group related fruit levels

Keep identical fruits and nearby evolution levels close enough to create follow-up merges.

Watch all three upcoming fruits

The queue lets you plan several moves ahead instead of making every drop as an isolated decision.

Avoid narrow vertical towers

A tall stack can cross the danger line even when the other side of the container still has empty space.

Let moving fruits settle

A fruit may roll into a better position or trigger a delayed merge. Do not rush the next drop when the pile is still moving.

Use the sides carefully

Larger fruits can form a stable base near the edges, but do not trap small fruits permanently behind them.

Avoid these layouts

Common Beginner Mistakes

Dropping everything in the center

This creates a tall central pile. Spread early fruits across useful merge zones while preserving a central path.

Ignoring the next-fruit queue

A good position for the current fruit may be poor for the next two. Check the queue before committing.

Building on one side only

A one-sided tower reaches the danger line quickly. Use the full width of the container without separating matching fruits.

Trapping small fruits

Small fruits hidden beneath large ones may never reach their match. Keep likely pairs accessible.

Dropping too quickly

Fast repeated drops reduce planning time and may interrupt a natural roll or chain merge.

Chasing one merge at any cost

Forcing a single merge can create a dangerous tower. Sometimes a safer temporary placement is the better move.

Can different fruits merge?

No. Only two fruits of the same type can merge into the next fruit in the evolution chain.

How do I move the fruit on mobile?

The game ends when settled fruits remain above the danger line for too long.

Does Fruit Merge Online save my best score?

Yes. Your best score, recent runs, and selected music preference are stored locally in your browser.

No. Fruit Merge Online runs directly in a modern web browser on desktop and mobile devices.
