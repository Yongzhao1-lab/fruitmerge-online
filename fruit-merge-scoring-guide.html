Core scoring rule

How Does Fruit Merge Scoring Work?

Points are added when two identical fruits touch and combine. The award is based on the new fruit created by the merge, not the two fruits that disappear.

For example, two Cherries create one Strawberry. Strawberry has a score value of 10, so that merge adds 10 points. Two Strawberries create a Grape and add 20 points. The pattern continues through the complete evolution chain.

Merge award = score value of the new fruit Example: 2 Apples → 1 Peach → +160 points
Important: dropping a fruit does not by itself add the listed score. The score is earned when a merge creates the next fruit. Cherry has a stored value of 5, but it is the starting level and is not normally created by another merge.

Understanding cumulative points

How Full-Chain Score Is Calculated

Every level doubles in value, but building a high-level fruit also requires all the lower merges below it. That is why the cumulative total grows faster than the final merge award alone.

Full-chain total = (fruit level − 1) × fruit score value For Dragon Fruit: (11 − 1) × 5,120 = 51,200 points

Fast score bursts

How Chain Merges Affect Your Score

A chain merge happens when one merge immediately creates the match for another. Each successful merge is scored separately. There is no separate hidden combo multiplier in the current scoring system, but several normal awards can arrive within a very short time.

Imagine that an Apple is already touching another Apple. If a new Orange merge creates the second Apple in the correct position, the board can award 80 points for the Apple and then 160 points for the Peach, producing a 240-point burst.

Chain total = award 1 + award 2 + award 3 + … Example: Apple + Peach chain = 80 + 160 = 240 points

Build connected pairs

Keep matching or near-matching fruits close enough that one merge can feed the next level.

Read the next-fruit queue

Use all three previewed fruits to prepare a sequence instead of planning only the current drop.

Protect the landing area

A chain is valuable only when the new larger fruit has enough room to settle safely.

Do not force every chain

A large point burst is not worth creating a tall tower that immediately reaches the danger line.

What Different Score Milestones Mean

A score is the sum of all merges completed during the run. It does not identify one exact board state, but these milestones help show how much high-level merging has occurred.

Turn merges into points

How to Score Higher Without Losing Board Control

Prioritize repeatable merge lanes

One safe merge is useful. A lane that can accept the next matching fruit is more valuable over the full run.

Keep high-value pairs accessible

Do not bury a Peach or Pineapple behind unrelated small fruits. Large blocked pairs delay your biggest awards.

Use small merges to lower the board

Small awards still matter because they remove two objects and often open a path toward a later chain.

Choose survival over one risky award

A 1,280-point merge is not helpful if the new Grapefruit pushes the stack above the danger line and ends the run.

The best scoring strategy is therefore not simply “make the largest fruit.” It is to keep creating useful merges for as long as possible. A lower, organized board gives you more future scoring opportunities than a tall board built around one forced pair.

Questions and answers

Fruit Merge Scoring FAQ

How are points awarded in Fruit Merge?

Two identical fruits merge into the next fruit, and the game adds the score value of that new fruit. Two Cherries create a Strawberry and award 10 points.

Does dropping a fruit add points?

No. A normal drop does not add the fruit's listed value. Points are awarded when a merge creates a new fruit.

How many points does a Dragon Fruit give?

The final merge from two Melons to one Dragon Fruit awards 5,120 points. A complete theoretical chain from 1,024 Cherries totals 51,200 points across all merges.

Do chain merges have a combo multiplier?

No separate multiplier is used in the current system. Every merge receives its normal award, and those awards are added together during the chain.

Why can two runs with the same largest fruit have different scores?

Score depends on every merge in the run. One player may create more repeated mid-level fruits, while another reaches the same largest fruit through a shorter route using fruits supplied by the queue.

Is the full-chain total the score I will always have when I reach that fruit?

No. It is a mathematical reference that assumes the fruit was built entirely from Cherries. Real games can supply early fruit levels directly, and your total also includes other merges elsewhere on the board.
