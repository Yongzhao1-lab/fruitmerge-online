Play in your browser

Getting Started

Basic answers for new players using Fruit Merge Online on desktop, phone, or tablet.

What is Fruit Merge Online?

Fruit Merge Online is a free physics puzzle game. You drop fruits into the container, combine identical fruits into larger levels, earn points, and try to keep the stack below the danger line.

Is Fruit Merge Online free?

Yes. You can play for free in your browser. The current game does not require an account, payment, or subscription.

Do I need to download or install the game?

No. Open Fruit Merge Online in a modern browser and start playing. There is no separate app or installation file required.

How do I play Fruit Merge on desktop?

Move the mouse across the game area to choose the horizontal drop position. Click once to release the current fruit. Use the drop guide and next-fruit preview before each move.

How do I play Fruit Merge on mobile?

Tap the horizontal position where you want the fruit to fall. A single deliberate tap selects the position and releases the current fruit. Avoid rapid repeated taps while a fruit is still moving.

Can I play Fruit Merge in full screen on mobile?

Supported mobile browsers can use the game’s immersive or full-screen controls. Browser behavior may vary, especially inside social-media or messaging-app browsers, so opening the site directly in Chrome, Safari, or another current browser usually gives the best result.

Merges, points and survival

Rules and Scoring

Learn what can merge, when points are added, how chains work, and why a run ends.

Which fruits can merge?

Only two fruits of the same type can merge. Different fruit types may touch, roll, or block each other, but they do not combine.

How are points awarded?

Points are added when two identical fruits merge and create the next fruit. A normal fruit drop does not add points. See the complete scoring guide for every merge value.

Do chain merges have a combo multiplier?

No separate combo multiplier is used in the current scoring system. Each successful merge receives its normal point award, and all awards in the chain are added together.

Why can two runs with the same largest fruit have different scores?

Your total includes every merge made during the run. One player may create more repeated mid-level fruits, while another reaches the same largest fruit through a shorter route using fruits supplied by the queue.

Why did my game end?

The game ends when settled fruits remain above the red danger line for too long. A fruit briefly crossing the line may not end the run if it quickly falls or merges back into a safe position.

What does the next-fruit preview do?

The preview shows the upcoming fruit queue. Use it to plan the current drop, reserve a safe position for the following fruits, and avoid separating future matching pairs.

Fruit levels and final evolution

Fruit Progress and Dragon Fruit

Answers about the evolution order, the highest fruit, and the safest way to progress through larger levels.

What is the complete Fruit Merge evolution order?

The current order is Cherry, Strawberry, Grape, Orange, Apple, Peach, Pineapple, Watermelon, Grapefruit, Melon, and Dragon Fruit. The Fruit Evolution Guide shows the full level-by-level chain.

How do I get Dragon Fruit?

Merge two Melons to create Dragon Fruit. Build the Melons low on the board and keep a clear approach lane so the final pair can touch safely. The Dragon Fruit guide explains the complete route.

How many points does Dragon Fruit give?

The final merge from two Melons to one Dragon Fruit awards 5,120 points in the current scoring system.

Can two Dragon Fruits merge again?

No. Dragon Fruit is the final level in the current evolution chain and does not evolve into another fruit.

How many Cherries are needed for one Dragon Fruit?

A perfect theoretical chain requires 1,024 Cherries. In a real run, the queue may provide higher starting fruit levels, so you may not need to drop that many Cherries yourself.

What is the best way to build larger fruits?

Keep related fruit levels near each other, build a stable low base, protect the center drop lane, and use the queue before every move. The strategy guide covers board zones, recovery, and chain planning.

Local records and technical help

Saved Scores, Sound and Troubleshooting

Find out where records are stored and what to try when the game, controls, audio, or saved data do not behave as expected.

Does Fruit Merge Online save my best score?

Yes. Your best score, recent runs, and selected music preference are stored locally in your browser.

Does my score sync between devices?

No. The current version uses local browser storage, so scores do not automatically sync between phones, computers, browsers, or browser profiles.

Why did my saved score disappear?

Local records can be removed when browser site data is cleared, private browsing is used, storage permission is restricted, or you switch to another browser or device.

Does restarting delete my best score?

Restart begins a new run. Your saved best score and local records normally remain unless the browser’s site data is cleared.

Why is there no music?

Background music is off by default. Use the music control to enable it. Some browsers also block audio until you tap or click the page once.

Why is the game not loading?

Reload the page, confirm JavaScript is enabled, and try a current version of Chrome, Safari, Edge, or Firefox. Opening the site directly in the browser can also help when an in-app browser does not load the game correctly.

Why is the game not responding to taps or clicks?

Wait until the current fruit has been released, avoid rapid repeated input, and check whether a menu, share window, or browser gesture is covering the game area. Reloading the page usually clears a temporary input problem.

Where can I report a problem?

Use the Contact page and include your device, browser, screen size, what happened, and the steps that caused the problem. A screenshot or short screen recording is especially helpful.
