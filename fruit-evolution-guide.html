Understanding the progression

Why Every Evolution Level Becomes Harder

×2

Requirements double

Each level requires twice as many base Cherries as the level before it.

+Size

The board becomes tighter

Higher-level fruits use more physical space and are harder to move after they settle.

+Score

Late merges are more valuable

The merge value doubles at every level, rewarding successful long chains.

Practical lesson: the evolution chain is not only about earning points. Every larger fruit changes the shape of the board and reduces the number of safe landing positions.

Questions and answers

Fruit Evolution Guide FAQ

What is the complete Fruit Merge evolution order?

Cherry, Strawberry, Grape, Orange, Apple, Peach, Pineapple, Watermelon, Grapefruit, Melon, and Dragon Fruit.

How many Cherries are needed for one Dragon Fruit?

A perfect theoretical chain requires 1,024 Cherries to create one Dragon Fruit.

Which merge gives the most points?

Creating Dragon Fruit gives the highest single merge value: 5,120 points.

Why are higher fruit levels harder to manage?

Higher-level fruits have larger logical radii, occupy more space, and can block smaller fruits or the central landing lane.

Can the final Dragon Fruit merge again?

No. Dragon Fruit is the final level in the current evolution chain.
